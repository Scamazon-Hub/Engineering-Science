Problem 1
Parallel Plate Capacitor Design

A parallel plate capacitor is required to be designed using polyethylene (PE) as the filler between the plates with a dielectric constant ($\epsilon_r$) of $2.25$. The capacitor is constructed with circular plates of radius ($r$) $27 \, \text{mm}$.

Given Data:

Dielectric constant ($\epsilon_r$) 2.25
Plate radius ($r$) 27 mm
Vacuum permittivity ($\epsilon_0$) 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m
π value 3.142
Required capacitance ($C$) 30 pF

Objective: Calculate the gap required between the plates ($l$) to give the component a capacitance of $30 \, \text{pF}$ to one decimal place.

Polyethylene
εr = 2.25

Plate Separation: l = ?

Plate Area: A = πr²

Step 1: Capacitance Formula for Parallel Plate Capacitor

\[ C = \frac{\epsilon_0 \epsilon_r A}{l} \]

Where $A$ is the plate area and $l$ is the plate separation

Step 2: Calculate Plate Area

\[ A = \pi r^2 = 3.142 \times (0.027)^2 \]

\[ A = 3.142 \times 0.000729 = 0.00229 \, \text{m}^2 \]

Step 3: Rearrange Formula for Plate Separation

\[ l = \frac{\epsilon_0 \epsilon_r A}{C} \]

Step 4: Substitute Values and Calculate

\[ l = \frac{(8.85 \times 10^{-12}) \times 2.25 \times 0.00229}{30 \times 10^{-12}} \]

\[ l = \frac{4.56 \times 10^{-14}}{30 \times 10^{-12}} = 0.00152 \, \text{m} \]

\[ l = 1.52 \, \text{mm} \]

Solution

l = 1.5 mm

The required plate separation is 1.5 mm to achieve 30 pF capacitance

Problem 2
Elastic Moduli Calculations

Given that a sample of mild steel has a Young's modulus ($E$) of $211.9 \, \text{GPa}$ and a bulk modulus ($K$) of $169.2 \, \text{GPa}$.

Given Data:

Young's modulus ($E$) 211.9 GPa
Bulk modulus ($K$) 169.2 GPa

Young's Modulus (E)

211.9 GPa

Measures resistance to linear deformation

Bulk Modulus (K)

169.2 GPa

Measures resistance to volumetric deformation

Part a: Calculate Poisson's Ratio ($\nu$)

\[ \nu = \frac{3K - E}{6K} \]

\[ \nu = \frac{3 \times 169.2 - 211.9}{6 \times 169.2} \]

\[ \nu = \frac{507.6 - 211.9}{1015.2} = \frac{295.7}{1015.2} = 0.291 \]

Part b: Calculate Shear Modulus ($G$)

\[ G = \frac{E}{2(1 + \nu)} \]

\[ G = \frac{211.9}{2(1 + 0.291)} = \frac{211.9}{2 \times 1.291} \]

\[ G = \frac{211.9}{2.582} = 82.1 \, \text{GPa} \]

Solutions

ν = 0.291
G = 82.1 GPa
Problem 3
Tensile Test Analysis (Polycarbonate)

A tensile test is performed on a $4 \, \text{mm}$ thick sample of polycarbonate (PC) of width $25 \, \text{mm}$. The sample was tested to fracture at room temperature and which point the sample had a total length of $81 \, \text{mm}$ and the force measured was $19 \, \text{kN}$.

Given Data:

Sample thickness 4 mm
Sample width 25 mm
Original length ($L_0$) 75 mm
Fracture length ($L_f$) 81 mm
Fracture force ($F$) 19 kN

Part a: Calculate Stress at Failure ($\sigma$)

\[ \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \]

Cross-sectional area: $A = \text{thickness} \times \text{width}$

\[ A = 4 \times 10^{-3} \times 25 \times 10^{-3} = 100 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2 \]

\[ \sigma = \frac{19 \times 10^3}{100 \times 10^{-6}} = 190 \times 10^6 \, \text{Pa} = 190 \, \text{MPa} \]

Part b: Calculate Fracture Strain ($\epsilon$)

\[ \epsilon = \frac{L_f - L_0}{L_0} \]

\[ \epsilon = \frac{81 - 75}{75} = \frac{6}{75} = 0.08 \]

Part c: Calculate Modulus of Elasticity ($E$)

\[ E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon} \]

\[ E = \frac{190 \times 10^6}{0.08} = 2.375 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa} = 2.38 \, \text{GPa} \]

Solutions

σ = 190.00 MPa
ε = 0.08
E = 2.38 GPa
Problem 4
Composite Material (Graphite-Epoxy)

A composite material of graphite-filled epoxy is designed with a mixture of $60\%$ of graphite by weight. Given the densities and Young's moduli of the constituent materials.

Material Properties:

Graphite density ($\rho_g$) 1.75 g/cm³
Graphite Young's modulus ($E_g$) 224 GPa
Epoxy density ($\rho_e$) 1.54 g/cm³
Epoxy Young's modulus ($E_e$) 3.5 GPa
Graphite weight fraction 60%
60% Graphite

Graphite

40% Epoxy

Epoxy

Part a: Calculate Composite Density ($\rho_{total}$)

\[ \rho_{total} = \frac{1}{\frac{w_g}{\rho_g} + \frac{w_e}{\rho_e}} \]

Where $w_g = 0.6$, $w_e = 0.4$

\[ \rho_{total} = \frac{1}{\frac{0.6}{1.75} + \frac{0.4}{1.54}} \]

\[ \rho_{total} = \frac{1}{0.3429 + 0.2597} = \frac{1}{0.6026} = 1.66 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \]

Part b: Calculate Volume Fraction of Epoxy

Volume fraction: $V_e = \frac{w_e / \rho_e}{w_g / \rho_g + w_e / \rho_e}$

\[ V_e = \frac{0.4 / 1.54}{0.6 / 1.75 + 0.4 / 1.54} \]

\[ V_e = \frac{0.2597}{0.3429 + 0.2597} = \frac{0.2597}{0.6026} = 0.431 = 43.10\% \]

Part c: Calculate Composite Young's Modulus ($E$)

Using rule of mixtures: $E = V_g E_g + V_e E_e$

\[ V_g = 1 - V_e = 1 - 0.431 = 0.569 \]

\[ E = (0.569 \times 224) + (0.431 \times 3.5) \]

\[ E = 127.46 + 1.51 = 128.97 \approx 129 \, \text{GPa} \]

Solutions

ρtotal = 1.66 g/cm³
Vepoxy = 43.10%
E = 129 GPa
Problem 5
Resistor Network Analysis

Considering the resistor network with a $9 \, \text{V}$ power source and resistors: $R_1 = 39 \, \Omega$ (series), $R_2 = 560 \, \Omega$ and $R_3 = 10 \, \Omega$ (parallel branch 1), $R_4 = 22 \, \Omega$ (parallel branch 2).

Circuit Diagram: Series-Parallel Network

R1 (39Ω) → Parallel Branch (R2=560Ω || R3=10Ω) → R4 (22Ω)

Part a: Calculate Total Resistance ($R_T$)

Parallel combination of R2 and R3:

\[ R_{23} = \frac{R_2 R_3}{R_2 + R_3} = \frac{560 \times 10}{560 + 10} = \frac{5600}{570} = 9.82 \, \Omega \]

Total resistance: $R_T = R_1 + R_{23} + R_4$

\[ R_T = 39 + 9.82 + 22 = 70.82 \, \Omega \]

Part b: Calculate Current through R4 ($i_{R4}$)

Total current: $I_T = \frac{V}{R_T} = \frac{9}{70.82} = 0.127 \, \text{A}$

Since R4 is in series with the source, $i_{R4} = I_T = 0.127 \, \text{A} = 127 \, \text{mA}$

Part c: Calculate Voltage across R3 ($V_{R3}$)

Voltage across parallel combination: $V_{23} = I_T \times R_{23}$

\[ V_{23} = 0.127 \times 9.82 = 1.25 \, \text{V} \]

Since R3 is in parallel with R2, $V_{R3} = V_{23} = 1.25 \, \text{V} = 1250 \, \text{mV}$

Solutions

RT = 70.82 Ω
iR4 = 127.00 mA
VR3 = 1250.00 mV
